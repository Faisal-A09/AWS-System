// 01 — INTRODUCTION

Why both systems matter

This facility runs on two engineering systems that look unrelated but share one trait: the plant cannot operate safely or properly without either. The RO system handles water treatment, purification, and the supply of both drinking water and washing water. The firefighting system protects life and assets. They serve different purposes — the RO system focuses on water quality and distribution, the firefighting system on life safety and asset protection.

// 03 — DRINKING WATER

Drinking water distribution

Treated water stored in the RO Tank is pumped out by four drinking water pumps and given a final polish — carbon filtration, fine filtration, and UV disinfection — before reaching the building.

FLOW BRO Tank → 4 Pumps → Carbon Filter → Cartridge Filter → UV → Drinking Network
// 04 — WASHING WATER

Washing water system

The washing water line follows a simpler path: four pumps push water from the Washing Water Tank through sand filtration before it serves the facility for washing and general use.

// 08 — TAKEAWAY

One facility, specialized water lines

The facility water system is divided into specialized tanks and treatment lines, each with a clear job.

RO System
Treatment, drinking water & washing water.

From the NWC source through four tanks, the RO line and its distribution stages deliver clean drinking water and reliable washing water — while protecting the membranes that make it all possible.

Firefighting System
Emergency water for fire protection.

A dedicated tank and a layered pump sequence keep firefighting water available at all times — even if power fails — focused entirely on life safety and asset protection.
